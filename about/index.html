About Sam

Sam is a web developer, IT professional, woodworker and audio engineer. He specializes in custom software development and web hosting, using a variety of open source technologies. He has a passion for the endless pursuit of practical, elegant code.

Sam has over 10 years of experience building and supporting custom web software for organizations large and small. He has developed everything from youth sports apps to customized CRMs to large-scale customer loyalty programs with users in the millions.

His formative years were spent learning about the value of hard work within his family ranching business in Wyoming. He learned to solve problems and be resourceful, traits he puts to use for the benefit of businesses large and small.

Sam's degree is in Music Technology, and he loves fly fishing and designing custom furniture. He lives near Austin, Texas with his wife and business partner Kristi, their son, and two dogs who lack cattle to herd and so have taken to following us around herding all of our code.

About Kristi

Kristi is co-owner of Sam Heuck Web Development.

Her academic degrees are in music (DMA in Music Composition from the University of Texas at Austin). As a music professor, she founded the Music Technology program at Montana State University, building a new major for aspiring composers and sound engineers, while also managing technology and web applications for the music school.

As SongInvention, she produces and engineers custom music on commission to celebrate and commemorate special events. Long before that, she made happy synthpop under the name Opposite Day.

Since 2018, personal and family experience with health care policy led Kristi to join the National Council on Independent Living, where she chairs a workgroup under the Advocacy and Public Policy Committee. She was honored to be a recipient of the 2023 Corey Rowley National Advocacy Award, for efforts organizing access to health care for people with disabilities.
